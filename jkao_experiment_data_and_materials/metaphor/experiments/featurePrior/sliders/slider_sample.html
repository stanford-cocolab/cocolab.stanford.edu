Find the Biased Word!

Instructions:

  • A biased statement is one that does NOT represent fairly all the views on a subject. Examples of biased language include opinions or contested assertions that are stated as facts, facts that are presented as opinions, judgmental language, a minority view that is presented as the majority one, etc.
  • For example, the word renowned makes the following statement biased:

    (1)          The New York Times is an internationally renowned daily newspaper published in New York City and distributed in the United States and many other nations worldwide.

    because renowned implies that, apart from very well known, the New York Times is widely honored and acclaimed. This is framed from a US perspective. A word like "known" would make the statement more neutral.
  • Another example of a biased statement is the following:

    (2)          Kuypers claimed that the mainstream press in America tends to favor liberal viewpoints.

    The verb claimed casts doubt on Kuypers' statement, subtly implying that what he said is not true. A verb like "stated" would not introduce this bias.
